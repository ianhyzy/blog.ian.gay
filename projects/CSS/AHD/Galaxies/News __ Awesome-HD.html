General Security - Recent Attacks - posted 1 month, 1 week ago
As you may be aware there have been a number of attacks on the torrent community in recent times. Ranging from DDoS to hack attempts to peer leaking attacks, these attacks are bad, they affect not only the sites themselves but you the users. At the end of the day if a site is down due to a DDoS its you the user that can't use it, if peers leak into public you the user may get a DMCA notice.

It is because of these attacks that once again we are having to step up our security. We have made changes to the way our torrent uploads happen to help mitigate our torrents being upload to other trackers, this however has a downside to you the user in that when you upload a torrent you will need to download the torrent from the site before you will be able to seed it.

We also would like to remind you that you should be using a unique password for each and every site. For more info on password security i will direct you to this thread. Also i highly recommend that you turn on Two Factor Authentication on your account, this adds an extra layer of security to your account requiring you to enter a time sensitive code to be able to access the site.

Hopefully the attacks on sites can be put to an end soon and we can all get back to the reason we are here in the first place.
